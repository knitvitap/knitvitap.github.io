About KNIT

Human empowerment might be a better solution rather than empowering a gender, a caste, or a religion.

- Akshat Shrivastava

Gender inequalities and human empowerment are some of the thought-provoking perspectives that all of us have to keep in mind and we should have forums that can make everyone comfortable in sharing and seeing.

We hear many things like, ”Boys don't cry”, ”Women are sensitive”, ”Men cannot be depressed”, “Periods are an illness” and whatnot? We are sure you are tired of hearing generalized statements.

In some parts of our community, basic amenities like sanitary pads, good clothing, and clean drinking water are treated as a luxury. Despite being aware of this we turn a blind eye to it.

Our club, KNIT, is formed to bring awareness to how we look at society. We as a club want to give you space and want YOU to share your experiences. We bring to you this platform, where we as a team can give space for you to share and elevate the situation around us by organizing empowering talks and discussions.

Our Event Highlights

Shubharambh

24 OCT 2020

With Shubharambh KNIT kicked off it’s journey with just one goal that was to unify everyone from various social and economic backgrounds and to break the shackles surrounding certain taboo subjects with a tweak of applicability and a fun element.

Roobaroo

13th Nov 2020

The fundamental rationale of the occasion was to have a satisfying genuine discussion or unambiguously a real to life discussion with lights from assorted expertises namely Dr Anupama Namburu, Prof Samuel Johnson, Dr Susmitha Shyamsundar, Dr Sibi Chakkaravarthy S and Prof Kanaka Himabindu Pottumuthu. The luminaries made sure that all the attendees took most of their tricks back home to tackle life outside the bubble of comforts.

Charcha

13th Feb 2021

Charcha was the first event of the HE-month series, which was intended to talk uproariously against speculations that are illustrated with regard to men. This was an interactive virtual round table session graced by Dr. Samir Gokarn and Dr. Salini Rosaline where various perspectives were brought into light that usually are looked down by people, attendees returned home with not just new perspectives but with rewards too.

Samavesh

28th Feb 2021

Even guys have problems, their mental health is a concern too, they’ve got a million questions running around their mind and an ocean of tears that are held back just because of the fear of society and its never ending questions. Samavesh being the second event of the HE-month series was one such place KNIT created where our speakers Athresh, Sudhendra, Tejeswar, Surya and Sunavo answered all those questions that were held back or left unanswered due to the fear of society with the guidance of our guests Mr. Manish Shekhar Dasgupta a Counselling Psychologist and Dr. Benarji Chakka the Dean of VSL, VIT-AP.

Kalakriti

From : 9th March 2021 To: 29th March 2021

Kalakriti, a virtual art fair, the first event of the SHE-month series. Every piece of work here was to appreciate the presence of every woman in an individual’s life. The responses were a collection of mesmerising art and craftwork and a few heartwarming poems that did not fail to overwhelm not just the team but every person who made it to our virtual art fair on Instagram.

Women’s day out

11th Apr 2021

The second event of the SHE-month series, was a goodie bag full of fun and splendour. This stress buster event was packed with games such as Pictionary, Housie, Spot the differences and many more. This day out made sure everyone who stepped into this virtual gaming arena left with a bright smile on their face.

Life through lens

25th Apr 2021

The academic year ended on an optimistic note busting a few very common myths, with our MYTH BUSTER-month’s heartwarming talk. Michelle Job, a photographer whose captures are soulful and credible graced our event to share her excursion from being a typical Indian youngster to a renowned photographer in Sweden which propelled the attendees on the best way to accomplish their dreams and look out for the most proficient method to continue with anything they desire.

Reunirse

30th Aug 2021

With every new year comes a bunch of fresh faces to join the crowd. Reunirse was one such shoutout to all the freshmen and a welcome back to all the old students, a social lounge where networking was the key to entry. An online treasure hunt was followed by a virtual round table that brought together people from all backgrounds with the same area of interest together.

Friends through your lens

8th November 2021

As we shook hands with our friend the Bioscope Club, we waited for students to catch the minutes they enjoyed with their friends and make IGTV videos or Reels. Like every competition being incomplete without rules, so was this, videos with the best screenplay, cinematography, and quality made it to the top three.

KNIT MUN 2022

From: 30th January 2022 To: 31st January 2022

A two day online MUN conference, with the caucuses running parallel to the objectives of KNIT. The committees on board were UNGA that reviewed the status of feminism under ICCPR and UNODC that debated on legalization of Marijuana.

    We organise discussions, debates, chill spaces, webinars on the topics which are not discussed usually.

    At the start of every academic year the recruitments will happen in which you can apply and be part of the club.
