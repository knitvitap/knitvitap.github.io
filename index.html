About KNIT

Human empowerment might be a better solution rather than empowering a gender, a caste, or a religion.

- Akshat Shrivastava

Gender inequalities and human empowerment are some of the thought-provoking perspectives that all of us have to keep in mind and we should have forums that can make everyone comfortable in sharing and seeing.

We hear many things like, ”Boys don't cry”, ”Women are sensitive”, ”Men cannot be depressed”, “Periods are an illness” and whatnot? We are sure you are tired of hearing generalized statements.

In some parts of our community, basic amenities like sanitary pads, good clothing, and clean drinking water are treated as a luxury. Despite being aware of this we turn a blind eye to it.

Our club, KNIT, is formed to bring awareness to how we look at society. We as a club want to give you space and want YOU to share your experiences. We bring to you this platform, where we as a team can give space for you to share and elevate the situation around us by organizing empowering talks and discussions.

Why join us?

Well, who doesn't want to live in a better world?

KNIT has come into existence in the August of 2020 by a group of junior year students at VIT-AP. Since then, we always have been striving for the betterment of student's mental health by conducting different kinds of events and have always been a safe space for them.

We intend to shed light on equality, domestic violence and all the taboos that are either unheard of or are not spoken of because of the fear of society and its age old and unrealistic opinions.

Just like the invisible winds that blow across the oceans create waves that are visible, KNIT hopes to bring a wave of change in an individual’s mindset through its swift winds of efforts.

Shubharambh

With Shubharambh KNIT kicked off it’s journey with just one goal that was to unify everyone from various social and economic backgrounds and to break the shackles surrounding certain taboo subjects with a tweak of applicability and a fun element.

Charcha

13th Feb 2021

Charcha was the first event of the HE-month series, which was intended to talk uproariously against speculations that are illustrated with regard to men. This was an interactive virtual round table session graced by Dr. Samir Gokarn and Dr. Salini Rosaline where various perspectives were brought into light that usually are looked down by people, attendees returned home with not just new perspectives but with rewards too.

Samavesh

28th Feb 2021

Even guys have problems, their mental health is a concern too, they’ve got a million questions running around their mind and an ocean of tears that are held back just because of the fear of society and its never ending questions. Samavesh being the second event of the HE-month series was one such place KNIT created where our speakers Athresh, Sudhendra, Tejeswar, Surya and Sunavo answered all those questions that were held back or left unanswered due to the fear of society with the guidance of our guests Mr. Manish Shekhar Dasgupta a Counselling Psychologist and Dr. Benarji Chakka the Dean of VSL, VIT-AP.

Kalakriti

9th March 2021 - 29th March 2021

Kalakriti, a virtual art fair, the first event of the SHE-month series. Every piece of work here was to appreciate the presence of every woman in an individual’s life. The responses were a collection of mesmerising art and craftwork and a few heartwarming poems that did not fail to overwhelm not just the team but every person who made it to our virtual art fair on Instagram.
